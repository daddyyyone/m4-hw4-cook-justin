About Donna Summer

Donna Summer, born LaDonna Adrian Gaines, was an iconic American singer and songwriter known as the "Queen of Disco" in the 1970s. She produced numerous hits, including "I Feel Love," "Last Dance," and "Hot Stuff." Although she was known as the “Queen of Disco,” her genres varied, contributing to her influence and longevity in the music industry.

Music Career

Donna Summer's music career soared in the 1970s when her disco dance songs became synonymous with the era. She was the first artist to have three consecutive double albums reach number one on the US Billboard chart. Even after disco faded from popularity, she continued producing music well into the 1980s and remained influential until her passing in 2012.
